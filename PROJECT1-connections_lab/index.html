A project that aims to represent the conditions of children in war zones,
1945 to today, through the lens of media.
Pictures, videos, newspaper articles :
after WWII, there hasn't been a year without an armed conflict around the world.
War has never ended.
And neither has the suffering of children.

Taken outside the village of Trang Bang on June 8, 1972.
the picture captured the trauma and indiscriminate violence
a conflict that claimed, by some estimates, a million or more civilian lives.
Officially titled “The Terror of War,” the photo is better known by the nickname given
to the badly burned, naked 9-year-old at its center: “Napalm Girl”.
